RULES:

1. SENTENCE SPLITTING
   - Split the source text into individual sentences. Each sentence becomes one flashcard (one CSV row).
   - Do not combine multiple complete sentences into one card.
   - Do not skip any sentences, except titles, headers, or fragments with no complete meaning on their own.
   - If a sentence is very short (fewer than 5 words) or purely functional (e.g., "Thank you.", a section heading, or a sentence fragment split by ellipses), merge it with the neighboring sentence it belongs to, so the card has complete meaning. Note in vietnamese_meaning (in parentheses) that sentences were merged.

2. key_infor (front side, part 1)
   - 2-3 short English words or phrases from the sentence, separated by commas, chosen to help the learner recall the full sentence.
   - If the sentence has 12 or more words, use 5-6 items instead of 2-3.
   - These must be actual words/phrases lifted from the sentence — not a paraphrased description of its meaning.

3. vietnamese_meaning (front side, part 2)
   - A full, natural, accurate Vietnamese translation of the entire sentence — not a word-by-word gloss.

4. english_meaning and english_meaning_separated_by_space (back side, part 1 — two versions)
   - english_meaning: the full original English sentence, unmodified, exactly as it appears in the source, with the 1-3 most valuable vocabulary items/phrases/collocations bolded using **double asterisks** (prioritize less common or more useful expressions over basic vocabulary; max 1-3 bolded spans regardless of sentence length).
   - english_meaning_separated_by_space: take the same sentence, split it into its individual words (split on whitespace; keep punctuation attached to the word it follows, e.g. "photography." stays as one token), then join those word-tokens into a single string using the delimiter "___" (three underscores) instead of spaces. Keep any bold markers attached to their word. Example: "I like photography." → "I___like___photography."

5. english_ipa and english_ipa_separated_by_space (back side, part 2 — two versions)
   - english_ipa: the American IPA transcription of the full sentence, wrapped in forward slashes, e.g. /aɪ laɪk ˈfoʊ.tə.ɡrə.fi/. Natural reductions are allowed (e.g., "a" → /ə/, "is" → /ɪz/), but each English word must correspond to exactly one space-separated IPA chunk — never merge two words into one chunk or split one word across two chunks. Count contractions ("I'm," "don't") as one word → one IPA chunk, without expanding them.
   - english_ipa_separated_by_space: take the same IPA chunks (without the enclosing slashes) and join them with the delimiter "___" instead of spaces. Example: "aɪ___laɪk___ˈfoʊ.tə.ɡrə.fi"

6. WORD-COUNT MATCHING (critical, applies across all four fields in rules 4-5)
   - The number of "___"-separated tokens in english_meaning_separated_by_space must exactly equal the number of "___"-separated tokens in english_ipa_separated_by_space, and both must equal the plain word count of the sentence in english_meaning.
   - Before finalizing each card, count the tokens in both separated columns and verify they match. If they don't, fix the IPA segmentation (not the English sentence) before moving on.

7. NUMBERING & ORDER
   - Number flashcards sequentially starting at 1 (card_id column), preserving the original order of the source text.

8. OUTPUT FORMAT
   - A single CSV file with header row: card_id,key_infor,vietnamese_meaning,english_meaning,english_meaning_separated_by_space,english_ipa,english_ipa_separated_by_space
   - Wrap every field value in double quotes to handle commas and special characters safely.
   - Aside from the intentional bold markers in english_meaning (and carried into english_meaning_separated_by_space), do not add any other markdown, headers, or commentary — just the data rows.
   - One row per flashcard, no extra rows.
